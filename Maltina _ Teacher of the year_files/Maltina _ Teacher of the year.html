PRIVACY NOTICE – Maltina Teacher of the Year Microsite (Site)

Nigerian Breweries-Felix Ohiwerei Education Trust Fund (NB-FOETF), an affiliate of Nigerian Breweries Plc, is committed to protecting and safeguarding the privacy of your personal information. This notice describes how we will use your personal information.

Why do we need your personal information?

We collect your personal information to process your application for the Maltina Teacher of the Year competition. Your information will be used to facilitate the selection process and will also be used to contact you when necessary to inform you of any developments as it concerns your application.

What personal information do we collect?

Your email address, name, phone number, date of birth, state of origin and location are collected from you. We may also request for additional information where clarification is required.

How will we use your personal information?

Your personal information will be treated in accordance with the highest privacy standards, in a confidential and secure manner, and it will be used solely for the purpose it was collected. Your information is only available to the third party Public Relations Agency acting on behalf of NB-FOETF. We will not share your information with any other third parties without your consent, except for lawful purposes, for instance, in the fulfilment of contractual obligations or where the law requires such information to be passed.

What are your rights?

You have the right to request for the deletion or correction of your personal data at any time. You may also request for information regarding any specific processing of your personal information. Please note that your information will be stored in our records after the selection process and competition for reference purposes, you may however at any time request for such information to be withdrawn or modified.

How will you provide consent?

By submitting an application on our site indicates that you consent to the collection, processing and storing of your personal information in line with the purpose stated above by:
- Providing your email address and by registering on the site; or
- Downloading the application form and sending a completed form to the designated email address.


If you have any questions or need clarification on the above, please send an email to privacy.nbplc@heineken.com.

Story so far...
As part of our commitment to our corporate philosophy of "Winning with Nigeria", Nigerian Breweries Plc has continued to take an increasingly active part in supporting education, amongst our other CSR focus point.
As testament to this, we set up the Nigerian Breweries-Felix Ohiwerei Education Trust Fund in 1994 with a startup capital of 100 million Naira to enable sustained quality education through structured intervention in the sector.
The fund has footprints across the six geographical zones in Nigeria at primary, secondary and tertiary levels of education. Through the Fund, we have constructed and furnished over 400 classrooms, sanitary facilities and libraries in 74 communities across the country. We have also granted scholarships to deserving students in tertiary institutions.
In 2015, the fund introduced the Maltina Teacher of the Year, a national competition to identify, honor and reward outstanding teachers in Nigeria.
Maltina Teacher of the Year which has recognised a total of 181 teachers since inception, is supported by the Federal Ministry of Education and other stakeholders in education including the Nigeria Union of Teachers(NUT), All Nigeria Conference of Principals of Secondary Schools (ANCOPPS) and Teachers Registration Council of Nigeria (TRCN).

Maltina Teacher of the Year Award aims to recognize and celebrate exceptional teachers in Nigeria. The initiative is open to teachers in ALL secondary schools across the country. There will be champions from each state of the federation and the FCT from which the overall winner will emerge - State Champions that emerge from the 36 States and the FCT are recognised and rewarded, the National Winners emerge from these State Champions after further assessment.


Hall of Fame
Mr. Ayiwulu Alaku
2022 Winner

Ayiwulu Alaku is a native of Nasarawa State, a Teacher at Federal Government College Jos since 2014, he teaches Physics and Basic Sciences.
He graduated from the University of Jos Nigeria with his first and second degree in Physics Education, and he is also a Fulbright TEA Alumni. Mr. Abanika Taiye
2021 Winner

Abanika Taiye, a Design and Technology/Basic Technology/Technical Drawing teacher at James Hope College, Agbor, Delta State, Nigeria. He emerged the winner of the prestigious Maltina Teacher of The Year award in 2021. He remains ever passionate and continues to give unwavering support to students and now shares best practices in education with educators.

Ms. Oluwabunmi Anani
2020 Winner

Oluwabunmi Ahoefa Anani hails from Lagos State, Nigeria and she is resident in Yola, Adamawa State where she teaches English Language and Literature at Concordia College Yola. Oluwabunmi who enjoys organizing programmes for children and teenagers, has executed a number of projects through her initiative: the Initiative for Educational Transformation (IET).

Ezem Collins, a teacher at the Royal Family Academy, Abuja has emerged the 2019 Maltina Teacher of the Year. Collins, a Civic Education and Christian Religious Studies teacher was declared winner of the prestigious honour at the grand finale held on Friday, October 18, at Eko Hotel & Suites, Victoria Island, Lagos.

Mr. Olasunkanmi Opeifa

Mr. Ariguzo Udochukwu

Mr. Imoh Enoh Essien

Essien who is a top-class professional caregiver, tutor and guide to children with special needs believes that children can best be served through the cooperative effort of teachers, principals, support personnel and their parents.

Mrs. Rose Nkemdilim Obi
2015 Winner

Rose Nkemdilim Obi is a Mathematics and Chemistry teacher at federal Government Girls' College, Onitsha, with 16 years teaching experience.
